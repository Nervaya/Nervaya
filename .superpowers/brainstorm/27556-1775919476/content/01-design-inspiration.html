Which design direction fits Nervaya best?

From the awesome-design-md collection, these companies have the most relevant design systems for a mental health & wellness platform. Pick the one that resonates most.

Warm & Literary

A — Claude / Anthropic Style

Mood: Literary salon, warm paper, editorial calm

Parchment canvas (#f5f4ed), terracotta accent (#c96442), warm grays with yellow-brown undertones, serif headlines, generous line-height. Feels like reading a book — unhurried, trustworthy, human-first. Ring-based shadows instead of drop shadows.

Best for: Conveying trust, warmth, and intellectual calm — perfect for therapy and wellness.

Clean & Warm Minimal

B — Notion Style

Mood: Blank canvas, warm neutrals, approachable minimalism

Pure white canvas with warm gray alternation (#f6f5f4), single blue accent (#0075de), ultra-thin whisper borders, multi-layer soft shadows. Compressed sans-serif headlines. Feels like quality paper — tactile, analog warmth.

Best for: Clean productivity feel, organized wellness experience.

Premium & Precise

C — Stripe Style

Mood: Fintech luxury, engineered precision, purple gradients

Signature purple (#533afd) with deep navy, weight-300 light headlines, blue-tinted multi-layer shadows, gradient backgrounds. Feels simultaneously technical and luxurious. Closest to Nervaya's current purple identity but elevated.

Best for: Premium positioning, trust through sophistication — keeps Nervaya's purple DNA.

Ultra-Minimal Dark

D — Linear Style

Mood: Dark precision, purple accent, ultra-clean hierarchy

Near-black surfaces with soft purple accent (#5B63D3), tight typography, minimal UI chrome, precise spacing. Feels futuristic and efficient. Similar to Nervaya's current dark homepage but with more restraint and polish.

Best for: Tech-forward wellness, modern sleep-tech positioning.
